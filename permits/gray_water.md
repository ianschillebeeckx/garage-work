## Regulatory Notes

Gray water rules vary significantly by jurisdiction:

**If in California:**
- Laundry-to-landscape (CPC 1502.1): washing machine gray water to subsurface irrigation may NOT require a permit if under 250 gallons/day and following prescribed standards
- Bath/shower diversion typically DOES require a permit (it's a "simple" gray water system under CPC Chapter 15)
- Must not pool or run off property
- Must go to subsurface irrigation (mulch basins)
- Cannot be stored for more than 24 hours

**General requirements (most jurisdictions):**
- 3-way diverter valve required (to switch between sewer and gray water)
- No cross-connection with potable water
- Gray water cannot contact edible parts of food crops
- System must be able to drain to sewer as backup
