## Seismic Plywood — Building Permit (OTC)

**Source:** https://www.sf.gov/information--projects-eligible-over-counter-otc-permit

SF.gov lists the following as OTC-eligible:
> "Bolting down existing foundation and/or adding plywood panels to crawl space/1st floor garage area (not complying with DBI Information Sheet IS-09)"

This means:
- Adding plywood shear panels to a 1st floor garage wall **does require a building permit**
- But it's **OTC-eligible** — no plan review wait, handled at the permit counter
- Work that does NOT comply with DBI Info Sheet IS-09 goes through OTC
- Work that DOES comply with IS-09 may require in-house review (IS-09 covers the city's mandatory soft-story retrofit program — probably not applicable to a single-family home)

**Action:** Pull a building permit OTC at DBI when you go for the electrical permit. Same building (49 South Van Ness), different counter (2nd floor for building permits).

## Gray Water — Two Different Rules

### Washer gray water (laundry-to-landscape): NO PERMIT

**Source:** https://greywateraction.org/requirements-for-no-permit-systems-in-california/
**Also:** CA Plumbing Code 1502.1

No permit required for a laundry-to-landscape system in a 1-2 unit residence IF all 13 conditions are met:
1. 3-way diverter valve (clearly labeled) to switch between sewer and gray water
2. Water goes to landscape irrigation only
3. Gray water stays on your property
4. Discharged under 2+ inches of mulch (no surface ponding)
5. No pumps except the washer's built-in pump
6. No diaper wash water or hazardous chemicals
7. No cross-connection with potable water
8. No pooling or runoff
9. Not accessible to people or pets
10. Maintenance manual provided
11. Doesn't affect other building systems
12. Proper setbacks from property lines, wells, etc.
13. System is simple (no storage tanks, no treatment)

**SFPUC also offers a rebate** for laundry-to-landscape kits: https://www.sfpuc.gov/learning/conserve-water/graywater-laundry-landscape-rebate

### Bath/shower gray water: PERMIT REQUIRED

**Source:** Same as above — the no-permit exemption applies ONLY to clothes washer systems.

Bath/shower diversion is a "simple gray water system" under CA Plumbing Code Chapter 15 and requires:
- Plumbing permit from DBI Plumbing Inspection Division
- Contact: 415-558-6088 or DBI 2nd floor
- May require a system design showing 3-way valve, subsurface irrigation, setbacks

## Practical Approach

You can pull **three permits in one trip** to 49 South Van Ness:
1. **Electrical** (4th floor) — OTC, walk out same day
2. **Building** (2nd floor) — OTC for seismic plywood, likely same day
3. **Plumbing** (2nd floor) — submit application for washer reroute + bath gray water

The washer laundry-to-landscape gray water needs **no permit** — just follow the 13 rules and install it.
