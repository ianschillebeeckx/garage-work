# Decision: Surge Protection Required for 2nd Subpanel

**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Source:** SF DBI electrical inspector at permit counter (verbal, during permit issuance)

## Finding

The DBI electrical inspector stated that since this is a **2nd subpanel** being added to the service, surge protection is required.

## Code Basis

Likely references **NEC 230.67** (Surge Protection, effective in 2020 NEC cycle):
> All dwelling unit services shall be provided with a surge-protective device (SPD).

The 2023 NEC expanded this. Additionally, **CEC/NEC 242.24** covers SPDs on feeders and branch circuits. The inspector's interpretation is that a second subpanel feeder requires its own surge protection.

## Options

1. **Type 1 SPD** — installed at the service entrance (main panel). May already be covered if the Powerwall has built-in surge protection.
2. **Type 2 SPD** — installed at the subpanel. Plugs into a 2-pole breaker slot. Common residential choice.
3. **Whole-house SPD** — typically Type 1 or 2, covers the entire panel.

A **Type 2 SPD in the new Siemens subpanel** is likely what the inspector expects. These are ~$50-100 and take up 2 breaker slots.
