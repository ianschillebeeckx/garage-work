# Decision: Homeowner Can Legally DIY All Electrical Work Including Subpanel

**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Applies to:** Subpanel installation, EV charger circuit, all electrical rough-in

## Finding

A homeowner in San Francisco can legally pull their own electrical permit and perform all electrical work — including subpanel installation — on their owner-occupied single-family dwelling. No licensed electrician is required by code.

## Sources

### California Business & Professions Code § 7044 — Homeowner Exemption
- **URL:** https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=7044&lawCode=BPC
- **What it says:** A homeowner improving their principal place of residence is exempt from contractor licensing requirements, provided:
  - Work is performed before any sale of the property
  - Homeowner has resided there 12+ months prior to completion
  - Exemption not used on more than 2 structures in any 3-year period
- **Last amended:** Jan 1, 2017 (SB 944)

### San Francisco Electrical Code § 89.120(E) — Homeowner's Permit
- **URL:** https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/san_francisco/latest/sf_building/0-0-0-84723 (Article 89)
- **Also:** https://media.api.sf.gov/documents/2025_SF_Electrical_Code.pdf (2025 edition)
- **What it says:** A permit for electrical work in or about a single-family dwelling may be issued to a homeowner. Work must be done by the permit holder or immediate family member. If work does not pass inspection, DBI can require a licensed electrician to correct under a separate permit.
- **No scope limitation:** The code does not restrict homeowner work by amperage, panel type, or complexity. Subpanels are not carved out.

### SF DBI Homeowner Permit Application
- **URL:** https://www.sf.gov/apply-electrical-permit
- **What it says:** Only owner-installers of standalone single-family dwellings may apply. Must bring photo ID proving ownership. LLCs are not eligible.

## What This Means for the Project

- Owner will pull the electrical permit personally at DBI (49 South Van Ness, 4th floor)
- Owner will perform all electrical work (subpanel, 240V EV circuit, all other circuits)
- A friend with 240V experience will advise informally — this is allowed since the homeowner is doing the work
- All work must pass DBI rough-in and final inspections regardless of who performs it
- If work fails inspection, owner must correct it or DBI can require a licensed contractor

## Risk Note

While legally permitted, subpanel installation is the highest-stakes electrical task in this project. The feeder from the main panel carries significant current. Having an experienced person present during that work is strongly recommended even though not required by code.
